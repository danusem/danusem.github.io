Southeast Asia

Vietnam

  • Vietnam is the world’s leading exporter of cashew nuts and black pepper.
  • The world’s second largest caves, the Son Doong caves are situated in Vietnam. The cave is so big that a Boeing 747 could fly through its largest cavern.
  • Motorbikes are the most common form of transport in Vietnam.
  • Snake wine is a common drink that is said to provide health and vitality to the person drinking it.
  • Approximately 86% of the country's residents are Vietnamese. There are over 50 other minority groups that reside in Vietnam.

Cambodia

  • The largest religious structure ever built is Angkor Wat (City Temple) in Cambodia. The site measures a massive 1,626,000m2 and was built by the Khmer King Suryavarman II from 1113-50 to honor the Hindu god Vishnu.
  • Cambodia’s flag is the only one in the world to feature a building.
  • You’ll find the largest freshwater lake in Southeast Asia in Cambodia.
  • Evidence from carbon dating suggests that Cambodia was inhabited as early as 4000 B.C.
  • Unlike most other cultures, Cambodians do not celebrate birthdays. In fact, many older people may not know the exact date of their birth.

Thailand

  • The Orchid Is Thailand’s National Flower.
  • The Country Is Made up of Almost 1,500 Isles.
  • The Mekong River Is Home to Over 1,300 Species of Fish.
  • Thailand is one of the most visited countries in the world.
  • Thailand used to be called Siam.

Laos

  • Laos is the only landlocked country in the whole of Southeast Asia.
  • Home to the largest waterfall in Southeast Asia.
  • Laos is the most bombed place in the world. Over two billion tons of bombs were dropped in Laos during the Vietnam War.
  • The official language of Laos is Lao, which sounds similar to Thai. Many of the older generation speak French, while the younger generation speak English as well as their native languages.
  • Laos’ biggest agricultural export is coffee.
